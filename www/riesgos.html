The repercussions of this magnitude of solar geomagnetic storms is: Widespread voltage control problems may occur, with potential for complete blackouts in some power systems, transformers may suffer severe damage, currents reaching hundreds of amperes may affect pipelines prolonged high frequency radio interferences for one of two days and bright Auroras may be visible as south of Florida and Texas and may can affect the geomagnetic field of earth.

G4 - Severe

Causes serious problems in electrical systems, visible auroras at even lower latitudes and interruptions in GPS. The reaction on earth is following this: Power systems operators may face significant issues, satellite navigation may be affected for hours. high frequency radio propagation would be sporadic and Auroras may be visible as far as south Aalabama and northern California.

G3 - Strong

It can generate fluctuations in the electrical network, failures in the GPS or visible auroras in slightly low auroras. The result on earth of these solar storms are: the satellites intermittent issues with navigation and low-frequency radio navigation may occur, voltage corrections may be required in power systems, protection devices may trigger false alarms and Auroras may be visible as far south as Washington, Oregon and Illinois. (No human damage).

G2 - Moderate

Has larger auroras than usual. It has minor damage to satellites orbiting the Earth. The consequences of these geomagnetic storms on earth are: power systems at high latitudes may experience voltage alarms, prolonged storms may cause damage to transformers, high frequency radio interferences and may be visible farther south, reaching the northern U.S. border. (No human damage).

G1 - Minor

They are the weakest storms, causing mild effects on power grids and visible auroras at high latitudes. The consequences of these solar storms are weak fluctuations may occur in power grids, minor impact on satellite functioning and may be visible Auroras in Canada and Alaska. (No human damage).
